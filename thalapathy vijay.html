Thalapathy Vijay

Thalapathy Vijay, whose full name is Joseph Vijay Chandrasekhar, is one of the biggest stars in Tamil cinema and a major cultural figure in South India. Born on June 22, 1974, in Chennai to filmmaker S. A. Chandrasekhar and singer Shoba Chandrasekhar, he entered the film industry as a child actor in his father’s movies. He made his debut as a lead actor in Naalaiya Theerpu (1992) at just 18. Vijay’s early hits like Poove Unakkaga (1996) and Kadhalukku Mariyadhai (1997) made him a popular romantic hero, while later films such as , ThirGhilli (2004)upaachi (2005), and Pokkiri (2007) established his “mass hero” image. In the 2010s, he delivered a string officially blockbusters including Thuppakki (2012), Kaththi (2014), Mersal (2017), Master (2021), Beast (2022), and Leo (2023), earning the nickname “Thalapathy”, meaning “commander” — a title now synonymous with his immense fan following.

His life commitment

Known for his charismatic screen presence, powerful dance moves, and socially charged roles, Vijay has become a symbol of determination and leadership for millions. He has received numerous awards, including the Kalaimaamani in 1998 and an honorary doctorate in 2007. Off-screen, he is admired for his humility and philanthropy, with his fan clubs often engaging in welfare activities across Tamil Nadu. Married to Sangeetha Sornalingam since 1999, he has two children — Jason Sanjay and Divya Saasha.

His political life TVK

In 2024, Vijay officially launched his political party, Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK), marking his transition from cinema to public service. His upcoming and highly anticipated film, tentatively titled Thalapathy 69 or Jana Nayagan, is expected to be his final movie before dedicating himself full-time to politics. As one of India’s most beloved and influential actors, Thalapathy Vijay’s journey from a teenage debutant to a superstar and now a political leader represents the perfect blend of cinema, charisma, and social commitment.
